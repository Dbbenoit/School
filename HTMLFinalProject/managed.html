Managed vs. Unmanaged Hosting

What is managed hosting?

Back when we discussed dedicated hosting, one of the largest contributors to service costs was whether or not the plan was a managed or unmanaged hosting. The managed hosting services are like haveing a dedicated IT team to do the work of maintinging the technical aspects of your online presence. When working with a managed hosting plan, the provider of the service takes care of:

  • Server set up and configuration
  • Software updates
  • Server maintanance
  • Security patches
  • Information back-ups
  • Technical support
  • Backups
  • Performance optimization

Having the managed hosting can take care of a large portion of the work associated with webhosting, but may not be nessesary. Many organizations handle the responsabilities listed above in house with their own dedicated teams. The work on the physical infrastruture is still done by the server owner, such as hardware upgrades and power supply management, but the software upgrade, security and software troubleshooting would be done in-house.

There is not a one-size-fits-all framework that a buisness can use to determin if they should use managed vs. unmanaged hosting. It will depend on the expected traffic, site demands, complexity and funding. Generally, the larger the organization, the more likely they will be able to manage their own site. Mangaged hosting can be great for for compliance-driven sites, buisnesses without IT departments and time-strapped buisnesses. A larger organizations will generally have access to more funding to allow for a dedicated team of workers to handle the job. Finding competent employees can create it's own challenge. It can take alot of time and energy to find a team that can keep your website running reliably for buisness owners who otherwise work in a non-computer associated field.

Wordpress.com has a concise summary of when managed hosting can help the smaller user:

When is managed hosting the right choice?

I recommend managed hosting if you want a reliable website without taking on the technical overhead of running it.
WordPress.com users, for example, often choose managed hosting so they can focus on building their site and publishing — not troubleshooting updates or security issues.
Here's how managed hosting helps me day to day:
  • No surprise maintenance tasks: Routine updates and server work happen automatically.
  • Fewer disruptions: Issues are handled before you ever notice them.
  • Stable, predictable performance: No juggling settings or extra tools.
  • More time for real work: Publishing, designing, or growing your business takes center stage.
  • Peace of mind: You're not the one troubleshooting issues.

Managed Hosting

Benefits

  • Server is ready to use
  • Automatic Updates
  • Provider moniters and repairs problems
  • Provider installs all of the software
  • Allows you to focus on your buisness
  • Peace of mind that you're taken care of

Cons

  • Can be more expensive at smaller scales
  • Less flexability, like picking the O.S.
  • Might be overkill for basic sites, like Wordpress
  • Vendor lock-in

Unmanaged Hosting

Benefits

  • Genreally less cost
  • More control over software
  • Not vendor locked
  • Can be quicker to impliment changes
  • Peace of mind that you're in control

Cons

  • Minimal support
  • Can create a whole new department of employee
  • Must solve your own problems
