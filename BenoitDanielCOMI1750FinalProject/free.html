Free Web Hosting

Differences between free and paid hosting

Free webhosting usually includes limited storage and limited bandwidth. The storage included in a free service can be 1 to 5 gigabytes and bandwidth can be very low, causing a frustratinly slow site. A free hosting provider may or may not provide website building assistance, where paid providers often include templates, or more commonly now, A.I. assistance in site generation. URL's for free hositing will usually include some sort of branding, like the name of the provider after a dot, as well as advertisements peppered on banners and pop ups on the hosted site. The site manager will also likely have ads on the control panel provided for the site. SSL certificates are still usually included with free hosting, and if they arene't, they can be obtained though outside sources for no cost.

Common Limitations

  • Ads
  • Small storage
  • Bandwidth caps
  • Slower performance
  • Little support/A.I. support
  • Limited domain choice

Free providers and how are they free?

InfinityFree.com

InfinityFree.com is web host that has been in service for over 14 years. They seem to buck many of the limitations described above. They do not place ads on creators sites, they allow you to bring your own domain, and they have a near 100% uptime. They offer 5GB of disk space, free SSL certificates and unlimited bandwidth. Infinityfree does have ads on their own website and on the control panel to fund their services.

Byet.host

Byet.host is a webhost that states they have over 1 million sites. Started in 2005, they offer free webhosting, but also offer "premium" paid hosting aswell. They claim to be the fastest free host available, and include features such as 5 GB of storage, unlimited bandwidth, and free SSL certificates on their subdomains.

FreeHosting.net

FreeHosting.com offers free webhosting that allows you to bring your own domain name and has unlimited bandwidth. They provide up to 1GB of storage, and include one email account. If you do not have your own domain, you must purchase one through FreeHosting.com. Freehosting also charges for some one-time add ons, like script mailing functionality.
